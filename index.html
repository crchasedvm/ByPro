What is Silvafeed ByPro?

Silvafeed ByPro is not a source of protein, but a sophisticated rumen modulator. It's a blend of natural tannins from Chestnut and Quebracho wood that protects dietary protein from degradation in the rumen, increasing the supply of valuable amino acids to the animal. This core mechanism drives benefits in performance, health, and sustainability. The recommended dosage for dairy cows is typically 15-20 g/animal/day.

Rumen Stability: Preventing Acidosis & Bloat

By modulating rumen fermentation, ByPro helps prevent costly metabolic dysfunctions that impair animal welfare and productivity.

Acidosis Prevention

In high-starch diets, ByPro slows starch fermentation and promotes saliva production, preventing sharp drops in ruminal pH and maintaining a healthier rumen environment.

Bloat Prevention

By binding soluble plant proteins, ByPro disrupts the formation of stable foam that traps fermentation gases. In a trial with grazing cows, ByPro reduced the overall incidence of bloat by 13% and eliminated deadly cases.

Proactive Animal Health

ByPro offers a non-antibiotic strategy for health management through its diverse bioactive properties.

Anthelmintic Properties

In-vitro studies show ByPro extracts are highly effective at inhibiting the lifecycle of gastrointestinal parasites, offering a tool to combat anthelmintic resistance.

Gut Health & Pathogen Control

Tannins form a protective layer on the intestinal wall, reducing inflammation and diarrhea. They also show direct antimicrobial activity against pathogens like *Salmonella* and *Clostridium*.

Antioxidant & Anti-inflammatory

As potent antioxidants, tannins help mitigate metabolic stress, which is critical for high-performance animals like transition dairy cows, reducing the risk of diseases like ketosis.

The Science Behind the Effects

A deeper look at how ByPro modulates the rumen environment and the chemistry of its key components.

Rumen Microbiome Modulation

ByPro selectively inhibits certain bacteria, including hyper-ammonia-producers and starch-fermenters. This helps stabilize rumen pH and increases the ratio of *Firmicutes* to *Bacteroidetes* from 0.72 to 1.03 in trials, a biomarker associated with more efficient energy extraction from feed.

Fermentation Profile

Supplementation consistently lowers ruminal ammonia, a direct indicator of its protein-sparing effect. It also tends to lower the acetate:propionate ratio. This shift towards propionate, a precursor for glucose, indicates improved energetic efficiency, similar to the effect of ionophores.

Hydrolysable Tannins (Chestnut)

These are smaller molecules that can be broken down and absorbed. They are believed to contribute systemic effects like antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activity, but can be toxic at very high doses.

Condensed Tannins (Quebracho)

These are large, non-absorbable polymers whose action is confined to the gut. Their complex structure makes them highly effective at binding and precipitating protein, driving the primary rumen bypass and anti-bloat mechanisms.
